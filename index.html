D&D Classes Ranked D&D is a game that some of you may be familiar with, and if you are you may be familiar with the discussion of what class is best and what is worst. In this post I will go over my ranking of all the D&D classes, from worst to best (I will exclude subclasses).

13. Ranger. Now this is a common one but not without reason, excluding subclass features the
base features are very situational and in comparison, to other classes they fall flat.
The only good thing about base ranger is that in a survival setting they can be good for foraging that's about it.

12. Articifer. The artificer is the latest addition to the classes, it is very subclass dependant class
with not many core features that are combat useful and is mostly focused on infusions
(adding properties to items) and they are also a halfcaster with less spell slots.

11. Fighter. The fighter by itself is a very barebones class without its subclasses, only getting
more attacks and other minor combat focused abilities. Outside of combat the base fighter
is bad, lacking in both outside of combat and story elements.

10. Monk. Even though the monk is my favourite class I can't ignore the major flaws with its
base. But even if it lacks in combat, it has lots of story elements like in the later levels you
stop feeling the effects of old age and you don't need to eat or sleep. But it relies too much
on short rests to regain ki points.

9. Warlock. the warlock is a class like the monk, that relies on short rests to regain abilities. The
only reason that it is better than the monk is because they are a full caster, and has a few
realy good spells, like eldritch blast and counterspell.

8. cleric. The cleric is the holy man of the D&D classes, with their levels making them more in
tune with their god of choice, at higher levles being able to cause divine intervention on a whim (once every 7 days). Most will say that the cleric is the most versatile class, and
theye'd be right but that is only with subclasses.

7. Druid. Druids are basically just shapeshifters but only for animals. They are very mediocre
although they can have massive health pools at higher levels by turning into larger beasts,
they can also be very versatile by turning into animals with swim speeds and fly speeds.

6. Rouge. Playing a rouge will make you feel like an assassins creed protagonist, going in and
out of the shadows doing massive damage. But for real the rouge is a very fun class to play
and is a fair bit beginner friendly then some other classes.

5. Barbarian. The barbarian is a class summed up by one sentence, me shut off brain, me he hit
things hard. An extremely melee combat focused class, with most skills focused on health
and damage.

4. Bard. The bard is a half caster class, that will instantly turn you into a badass, with you being
able to kill people with insults and bring them to their knees in laughter. This is a half caster
done well, with the spellcasting ability complimenting the martial parts.

3. paladin. If the cleric is the holy man, then the paladin is the crusader, mixing different
flavours of smite in with their melee attacks. The paladin is also one of the best classes to combo with e.g. a wizard casts heat metal (5th level) on the paladin's sword, then the
paladin uses divine smite and thundering smite on the blade and kaboom heaps of
damage).

2. sorcerer. The sorcerer is just a wizard who never needed to study a day in their life, they just
have it in their DNA. All in all, the sorcerer isn't as strong as the wizard, but they sure are
cooler. Blending pure caster with awesomeness.

1. wizard. If you know anything about D&D then you should probably know now that the
wizard is the best, you should also know why it's the best. Be a glass cannon sure, who cares
when you can cast WISH!! And bend space time to your will on a whim.
